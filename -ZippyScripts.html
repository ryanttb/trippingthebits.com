Zippy Scripts

2010-01-01

It is a very good practice to compile your production JavaScript. There are tools that do it for you such as YUI Compressor or Google's Closure Compiler. It's also very easy to check if your JavaScript is compiled: look at the file. If it's only a few un-commented lines with one to two character local variables you have either compiled your script or you are well beyond a ninja-level programmer.

However, they also say that the web server should send your compiled JavaScript to the web browser compressed. What does that mean? How do you do it? How can you check that it's being done?

Hopefully this article will help.
